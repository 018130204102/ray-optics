Tools

Ray

A single ray of light from two points

Beam

A parallel beam of light from a line-segment. The density is controlled by the "Ray density" slider.

Point Source

Light from a single point. The number of rays is controlled by the "Ray density" slider.

Mirror

Simulate the reflection of light on a mirror.

Mirror (Arc)

A mirror whose shape is part of a circle, which is defined by three points.

Ideal curved mirror

The idealized "curved" mirror which obeys exactly the mirror equation. The focal length (in pixels) can be set directly.

Glass (Half-plane)

Simulate the the refraction and reflection of light on a surface. The intensities are calculated from Fresnel equations and assuming unpolarized.

Glass (Circle)

Glass with circle shape, defind by its center and a point on the surface.

Glass (Other shapes)

Glass with any shapes constructed from line segments and circular arcs, including prisms and "spherical" lenses.

Glass (Ideal lens)

An ideal lens with obeys exactly the thin lens equation. The focal length (in pixels) can be set directly.

Blocker

A line-segment-shaped light blocker which completely absorbes the incident rays.

Ruler

A ruler from a point for zero and another point. The scale is in pixels.

Protractor

A protracter defined from center and another point for the zero direcrion. The scale is in degrees.

Views

Rays

Show the path of rays. The number of rays is controlled by the "Ray density" slider. When the density is high, they appears to be continuous.

Extended Rays

Show both the path of rays and its extension. Orange indicates backward extensions, and gray indicates forward ones.

All Images

Show the position of all images. Yellow points indicates real images, orange indicates virtual images, and gray (none in this picture) indicates virtual objects. Note that some images cannot be detected if ray density is too low.

Seen by observer

The blue circle is the observer, which is like an eye. The blue lines indecates the rays observed by it, and the orange points indecates the location of images.
